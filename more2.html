The Louisiana Purchase & Westward Expansion



The Louisiana Purchase was a purchase that had expanded the States territory. This arrangement was proposed by
the senate of France. Which has been looked upon by Thomas Jefferson and was agreed upon.
All of this took place in 1803, during Thomas Jefferson's presidancy. The purchase brought
828,000 square miles of territory from France, nearly doubling the size of the nation. Nearly
or all of 15 states were eventually created from the newly settled land deal, which is considered
to be one of Jefferson's most important achievements during his presidancy.

The Louisianna Purchase was also questioned whether or not to be constitutional, as per the beliefs of the Democratic-Republicans,
they were to follow the constituion strictly and word for word. Alongside that, there was no mention of
the president having the power to buy land in order to expand the territory of the nation.
Due to this, the purchase acknowledged the existance of implied powers of the federal Constitution.
