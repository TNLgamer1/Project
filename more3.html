Hamilton vs. Burr



The Hamilton v.s. Burr displayed rivalry between Hamilton and Burr. They both were in different political parties.
The election of 1800 had a lot to contribute to this. When Thomas Jefferson was running for president against Aaron Burr,
there was a draw. Hamilton does what he can do to change the fate of the president. Thomas Jefferson was elected
president and Aaron Burr as vice. In the following years, Thomas Jefferson along with Hamilton has attempted efforts
to keep Burr out of office. By 1804, Burr was officially out of office.

HOW Hamilton vs. Burr CONTRIBUTUES TO FREEDOM


This was the point in which the Federalist partry officially ended. Due to this, the Democratic-Republican party
was dominant. As the Federalists favored British relation, which is ironic due to the fact that the nation fought
for freedom from the British, having a Federalist America would simply undo all of that hard fought freedom.
